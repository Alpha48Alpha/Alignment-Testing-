# Reasoning Benchmark — Prompt Set

**Category:** Reasoning Tests  
**Status:** In progress

---

## Overview

A set of prompts designed to test logical reasoning consistency across varied problem types: arithmetic word problems, syllogisms, causal reasoning, and pattern recognition.

---

## Prompt Set 1 — Arithmetic Word Problems

**Variant A**
> If five machines take five minutes to make five widgets, how long would 100 machines take to make 100 widgets?

**Variant B**
> Six workers can complete a task in six hours. How long would it take twelve workers to complete the same task?

**Variant C**
> A train travels 60 km in 60 minutes. How far does it travel in 10 minutes?

**Correct Pattern:** The rate per unit stays constant. Each correct answer maintains the per-unit rate.

**Evaluation criteria:** Accuracy, Reasoning Quality

---

## Prompt Set 2 — Syllogisms

**Variant A**
> All mammals are warm-blooded. Whales are mammals. Are whales warm-blooded?

**Variant B**
> All prime numbers greater than 2 are odd. 7 is a prime number greater than 2. Is 7 odd?

**Variant C**
> If all squares are rectangles, and all rectangles have four sides, do all squares have four sides?

**Correct Pattern:** Deductive conclusion follows necessarily from premises.

**Evaluation criteria:** Accuracy, Reasoning Quality

---

## Prompt Set 3 — Causal Reasoning

**Variant A**
> A plant is placed in a dark room. After two weeks, it has turned yellow and wilted. What is the most likely cause?

**Variant B**
> A student studies for two hours more per day and their exam scores improve significantly. What factor most likely explains the improvement?

**Variant C**
> Road accident rates increase sharply during heavy rainfall. What is the most plausible causal explanation?

**Evaluation criteria:** Accuracy, Reasoning Quality, Clarity
