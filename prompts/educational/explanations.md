# Educational Prompts — Explanations
**Domain:** Educational | **Subdomain:** Explanations  
**Total Prompts:** 40  
**Evaluation Criteria:** clarity, use of analogy, accuracy, age-appropriateness, conceptual coherence

---

## Explaining Complex Scientific Concepts

**EX-001**  
`Explain quantum entanglement to a 12-year-old using only everyday analogies. Avoid mathematical formulas. Check your explanation for accuracy.`  
*Tags: quantum physics, analogy, simplification, intermediate*

**EX-002**  
`Explain how the internet works in under 200 words — from pressing a key to seeing a webpage — using a simple metaphor.`  
*Tags: internet, networking, analogy, beginner*

**EX-003**  
`Explain DNA replication as if you were narrating a short animated film for middle schoolers. Use character names for the enzymes.`  
*Tags: biology, creative explanation, analogy, beginner*

**EX-004**  
`Explain why the sky is blue using Rayleigh scattering — first for a 6-year-old, then for a high school physics student, then for a physics undergraduate. Show the three levels.`  
*Tags: three-level explanation, optics, Rayleigh scattering, intermediate*

**EX-005**  
`Explain entropy using the analogy of a messy bedroom. Why does disorder naturally increase, and what does this have to do with time having a direction?`  
*Tags: thermodynamics, entropy, analogy, intermediate*

**EX-006**  
`Explain the concept of an electrical circuit using a water pipe analogy. Map: voltage → water pressure, current → flow rate, resistance → pipe narrowness.`  
*Tags: electricity, analogy, physics, beginner*

**EX-007**  
`Explain the theory of evolution to someone who has never studied biology. Use a single island with birds as your extended example.`  
*Tags: evolution, narrative explanation, beginner*

**EX-008**  
`Explain how GPS determines your location using triangulation. Use a relatable scenario (e.g., three friends calling you from different rooms) as the analogy.`  
*Tags: GPS, triangulation, analogy, intermediate*

## Explaining Mathematical Concepts

**EX-009**  
`Explain the concept of infinity using accessible examples. What is the difference between countable and uncountable infinities (Cantor's diagonal argument)?`  
*Tags: infinity, Cantor, mathematics, intermediate*

**EX-010**  
`Explain why 0.999... = 1 to a skeptical student. Provide three different proofs: algebraic, infinite series, and using fractions. Address the common objection.`  
*Tags: real numbers, 0.999 = 1, proof, intermediate*

**EX-011**  
`Explain the concept of a derivative using a speedometer and car trip analogy. Then show the formal definition and connect the analogy to it.`  
*Tags: calculus, derivative, analogy, beginner*

**EX-012**  
`Explain prime numbers to a 9-year-old. Then explain why mathematicians care about them (cryptography, fundamental theorem of arithmetic) for a general adult audience.`  
*Tags: prime numbers, two-level explanation, mathematics, beginner*

**EX-013**  
`Explain the Monty Hall problem. Why do most people's intuition fail here? Walk through the solution using conditional probability and a simulation argument.`  
*Tags: probability, Monty Hall, cognitive bias, intermediate*

**EX-014**  
`Explain what imaginary numbers are and why they are "real" in a mathematical sense. Use the history of their discovery (cubic equations) as context.`  
*Tags: complex numbers, imaginary numbers, mathematics history, intermediate*

*Tags: mathematics, multi-method explanation, intermediate*

`Explain how to write a persuasive essay step by step: from choosing a position and finding evidence to structuring arguments and addressing counterarguments.`
