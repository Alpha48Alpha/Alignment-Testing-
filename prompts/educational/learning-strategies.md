## Memory & Retention

**LS-001**  
`Explain spaced repetition and the forgetting curve. How would you design a 4-week study plan for a difficult exam using spaced intervals?`  
*Tags: spaced repetition, forgetting curve, study planning, intermediate*

**LS-002**  
`Describe the active recall technique and why it outperforms passive re-reading for long-term retention. Give 3 concrete ways to implement it for any subject.`  
*Tags: active recall, retrieval practice, evidence-based, intermediate*

**LS-003**  
`What is the testing effect? Explain the research behind it and describe how self-testing can be built into a daily study routine.`  
*Tags: testing effect, self-testing, research-based, intermediate*

**LS-004**  
`Compare note-taking strategies: the Cornell Method, mind mapping, outline method, and charting. Which is most effective for which type of content?`  
*Tags: note-taking, study strategies, comparison, beginner*

**LS-005**  
`Explain interleaved practice vs blocked practice. Why does interleaving feel harder but produce better long-term learning? Give examples from mathematics and sports.`  
*Tags: interleaving, practice strategies, research-based, intermediate*

**LS-006**  
`Design a flashcard system for learning 500 vocabulary words in a foreign language. How would you structure the cards, schedule reviews, and track progress?`  
*Tags: flashcards, language learning, spaced repetition, beginner*

**LS-007**  
`What is elaborative interrogation? How does asking "why?" and "how?" about each fact improve encoding and retention?`  
*Tags: elaborative interrogation, deep processing, intermediate*

**LS-008**  
`Explain the generation effect: why generating answers (even wrong ones) before reading the correct answer leads to better memory than passive reading.`  
*Tags: generation effect, active learning, research-based, intermediate*
