# General Knowledge Prompts — Culture
**Domain:** General Knowledge | **Subdomain:** Culture  
**Total Prompts:** 40  
**Evaluation Criteria:** cultural sensitivity, depth of analysis, representation, cross-cultural comparison

---

## Literature

**C-001**  
`Analyze the theme of social class in Jane Austen's *Pride and Prejudice*. How does Austen satirize the marriage market of Regency England, and what does Elizabeth Bennet represent?`  
*Tags: literature, Austen, social class, British, intermediate*

**C-002**  
`Explain the concept of magical realism using Gabriel García Márquez's *One Hundred Years of Solitude* as your primary example. What is the purpose of blending the fantastic with the mundane?`  
*Tags: literary analysis, magical realism, Latin American, intermediate*

**C-003**  
`Describe Toni Morrison's narrative technique in *Beloved*. How does the non-linear structure and the presence of a ghost serve the novel's engagement with the trauma of slavery?`  
*Tags: literature, Morrison, narrative technique, African American, advanced*

**C-004**  
`Compare dystopian visions in George Orwell's *1984* and Aldous Huxley's *Brave New World*. Which method of social control — oppression or pleasure — is more prescient today?`  
*Tags: dystopian fiction, Orwell, Huxley, comparison, intermediate*

**C-005**  
`Explain the concept of the unreliable narrator. Give 3 examples from different literary works (e.g., *The Great Gatsby*, *Gone Girl*, *Life of Pi*) and explain how unreliability serves each story.`  
*Tags: narrative technique, unreliable narrator, literary analysis, intermediate*

**C-006**  
`Describe the Harlem Renaissance (1920s-1930s). Who were its key literary figures (Langston Hughes, Zora Neale Hurston), and how did this movement reshape African American cultural identity?`  
*Tags: Harlem Renaissance, cultural history, African American, literature, intermediate*

**C-007**  
`Analyze Shakespeare's *Hamlet* as a meditation on action, inaction, and mortality. What does "To be or not to be" reveal about Hamlet's philosophical crisis?`  
*Tags: Shakespeare, drama, literary analysis, intermediate*

**C-008**  
`Describe postcolonial literature. How do authors like Chinua Achebe (*Things Fall Apart*) and Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie use fiction to challenge Western narratives of Africa?`  
*Tags: postcolonial literature, Africa, narrative power, advanced*
