## Ethics & Moral Philosophy

**PH-001**  
`Explain utilitarianism (Bentham and Mill). Apply it to a trolley problem variant: is it morally permissible to divert a trolley to kill 1 person and save 5? What are the strongest objections?`  
*Tags: utilitarianism, trolley problem, ethics, intermediate*

**PH-002**  
`Explain Kant's categorical imperative in three formulations. Apply it to the question: "Is it ever ethical to lie?" What is the strongest objection to Kant's answer?`  
*Tags: Kantian ethics, categorical imperative, deontology, advanced*

**PH-003**  
`Describe Aristotle's virtue ethics. What is eudaimonia (flourishing)? How does the doctrine of the mean apply to specific virtues like courage?`  
*Tags: virtue ethics, Aristotle, eudaimonia, intermediate*

**PH-004**  
`Compare utilitarianism, deontology, and virtue ethics on the following scenario: a doctor has one healthy patient whose organs could save five dying patients. What does each theory recommend?`  
*Tags: comparative ethics, applied ethics, intermediate*

**PH-005**  
`Explain John Rawls's Veil of Ignorance and the two principles of justice. Why does the thought experiment support redistribution?`  
*Tags: political philosophy, Rawls, justice, advanced*

**PH-006**  
`Describe Robert Nozick's libertarian critique of Rawls. What is the entitlement theory of justice and the Wilt Chamberlain argument?`  
*Tags: libertarianism, Nozick, political philosophy, advanced*

**PH-007**  
`Explain Peter Singer's argument for global poverty reduction. Is there a moral difference between drowning a child in front of you and allowing a child to die of starvation abroad?`  
*Tags: ethics, Singer, global justice, applied, advanced*

**PH-008**

*Tags: moral relativism, metaethics, intermediate*
