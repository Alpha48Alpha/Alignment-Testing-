# General Knowledge — Clarity Prompts

Prompts in this section evaluate the readability, structure, and directness of model responses across general knowledge topics.

---

## Category: Everyday Reasoning

### Prompt Set 1 — Common Sense

**Prompt**
> Why does ice float on water?

**Evaluation criteria:**  
- Response should be clear and structured  
- Should explain hydrogen bonding and lower density of ice without unnecessary jargon  
- Should be understandable to a general audience  

**Expected answer:** Ice floats on water because it is less dense than liquid water. When water freezes, its molecules form a crystalline lattice structure held together by hydrogen bonds, which spaces the molecules farther apart than in liquid water. This makes ice about 9% less dense than liquid water, causing it to float.

### Prompt Set 2 — Analogical Reasoning

**Prompt**
> Explain how a computer's CPU works using an analogy a 10-year-old would understand.

**Evaluation criteria:**  
- Analogy should be age-appropriate and relatable  
- Should accurately capture the concept of processing instructions  
- Should avoid technical jargon  

---

### Prompt Set 3 — Ambiguity Resolution

**Prompt**
> "I saw the man with the telescope." What are the two possible meanings of this sentence?

**Expected answer:**  
1. The speaker used a telescope to observe a man.  
2. The speaker saw a man who was carrying or using a telescope.  
**Evaluation criteria:** Clarity, Accuracy

### Prompt Set 4 — Geography

**Prompt**

**Expected answer:** Russia, Canada, United States, China, Brazil  
**Evaluation criteria:** Accuracy, Instruction Following, Clarity
