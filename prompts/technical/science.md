# Technical Prompts — Science
**Domain:** Technical | **Subdomain:** Science  
**Total Prompts:** 40  
**Evaluation Criteria:** scientific accuracy, conceptual depth, real-world application, clarity

---

## Physics

**SC-001**  
`Explain Newton's three laws of motion with an everyday example for each. Then describe a situation where each law is the primary force at work.`  
*Tags: classical mechanics, Newton's laws, beginner*

**SC-002**  
`Derive the kinematic equation v² = v₀² + 2aΔx from first principles. Explain what each variable represents and when it applies.`  
*Tags: kinematics, derivation, intermediate*

**SC-003**  
`Explain the conservation of energy. A 2 kg ball is dropped from 10 m. At what height does it have equal kinetic and potential energy? Show all calculations.`  
*Tags: energy conservation, mechanics, intermediate*

**SC-004**  
`Describe the photoelectric effect and explain why classical wave theory failed to explain it. How did Einstein's photon model resolve the paradox?`  
*Tags: quantum mechanics, photoelectric effect, historical, intermediate*

**SC-005**  
`Explain Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle. Why does it represent a fundamental limit, not just a limitation of our instruments?`  
*Tags: quantum mechanics, uncertainty principle, advanced*

**SC-006**  
`Describe special relativity: time dilation and length contraction. Give a numerical example of a spaceship traveling at 0.8c and calculate the time dilation factor.`  
*Tags: special relativity, Lorentz factor, advanced*

**SC-007**  
`Explain how a nuclear fission reactor works. Describe the role of control rods, moderators, coolants, and containment structures.`  
*Tags: nuclear physics, reactor design, intermediate*

**SC-008**  
`Describe wave-particle duality using the double-slit experiment. What happens when a measuring device is introduced, and what does this reveal about quantum observation?`  
*Tags: quantum mechanics, wave-particle duality, advanced*

**SC-009**  
`Explain Maxwell's equations conceptually (no calculus required). How do they unify electricity and magnetism, and how do they predict electromagnetic waves?`  
*Tags: electromagnetism, Maxwell's equations, intermediate*

**SC-010**  
`What is entropy in thermodynamics? Explain the second law and why entropy always increases in isolated systems. Give a macroscopic and molecular-level explanation.`  
*Tags: thermodynamics, entropy, second law, intermediate*

## Chemistry

**SC-011**  
`Explain the difference between ionic, covalent, and metallic bonding. Give an example of each and describe the physical properties that result from each bond type.`  
*Tags: chemical bonding, properties, beginner*

**SC-012**  
`Balance the following combustion reaction: C₄H₁₀ + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O. Then calculate the theoretical yield of CO₂ from 58 grams of butane.`  
*Tags: stoichiometry, combustion, intermediate*

**SC-013**  
`Explain Le Chatelier's Principle. Apply it to the Haber process (N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃) to explain the optimal industrial conditions.`  
*Tags: equilibrium, Le Chatelier, industrial chemistry, intermediate*

**SC-014**  
`Describe how a catalytic converter reduces vehicle emissions. What chemical reactions occur, and what role does the platinum catalyst play?`  
*Tags: catalysis, environmental chemistry, applied, intermediate*

**SC-015**  
`Explain the concept of electronegativity and how it determines whether a bond is polar or nonpolar. How does molecular polarity affect physical properties like boiling point?`  
*Tags: electronegativity, polarity, intermolecular forces, intermediate*

**SC-016**  
`Describe the pH scale and the concept of acid-base chemistry. Calculate the pH of a 0.01 M HCl solution and a 0.001 M NaOH solution.`  
*Tags: pH, acids, bases, beginner*

**SC-017**  
`Explain oxidation-reduction (redox) reactions. How do you assign oxidation states and identify the oxidizing and reducing agents?`  
*Tags: redox, oxidation states, intermediate*

**SC-018**  
`Describe polymer chemistry: how monomers link to form polymers (addition and condensation polymerization). Give examples of polymers used in everyday life.`  
*Tags: polymers, organic chemistry, applied, intermediate*
